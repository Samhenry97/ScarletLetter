What's my opinion?

The Scarlet Letter has a very good spiritual moral to it, along with a suspenseful storyline. Hawthorne brings out the darkness and evil of sin. He talks of how much it can truly ruin a person's life. One sin commited one day can destroy or bring shame to the rest of one's life, and even generations to come. The plot of the story keeps the reader in suspense by not revealing who the man that sinned with Hester is until later in the story. There is a lot of foreshadowing as to who the man is, which is finally revealed after much waiting. The story's ending is fairly good, although not what most people would call "satisfactory". Overall, The Scarlet Letter is a great book to pick up for a school project, or even just for some spiritual help regarding sins.
