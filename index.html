What is it about?

The Scarlet Letter is about a woman named Hester Prynne. She lives in the Puritan town of Boston. Hester has committed adultery when her husband was presumed lost at sea, and is forced to wear the scarlet letter "A" woven into her clothing for the rest of her life as punishment. During her trial, her supposed long-lost husband shows up. The story is about how she has to live with the scarlet letter, and how she has to live in shame because of her sin. Her daughter Pearl even suffers consequences, as she is shunned by other children. Through all this shame, her husband tries to get revenge on the man who sinned with her.

What's the point?

Hawthorne is trying to make a point about adultery, and even sin in general. Both are bad things, and can definitely ruin a person's reputation for the rest of his life. It can even go to the point of ruining the honor of a family, and many times will last for generations to come.

What is the setting?

The Scarlet Letter takes place in Boston, Massachusetts in 1642, around the time of the Puritans.

Who wrote it?

Nathaniel Hawthorne wrote The Scarlet Letter.
